Chasing an overdue invoice is the most demoralizing part of freelancing. It shouldn't be.

You did the work. You delivered on time. You invoiced correctly.

Then... silence. 14 days past due. 21 days. You send a "just checking in" email that reads like you're apologizing for wanting to get paid.

The problem isn't that you're too nice. It's that nobody teaches freelancers how to follow up firmly without burning the relationship.

Here's what actually works:
- First reminder: warm, assumes oversight ("just flagging this in case it slipped through")
- Second reminder: factual, states the amount and new due date clearly
- Third reminder: firm, references your contract terms, signals next steps

Most clients pay after the first or second — they just needed a nudge that didn't sound desperate.
