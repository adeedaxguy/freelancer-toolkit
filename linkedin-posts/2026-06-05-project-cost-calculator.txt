Most freelancers undercharge — not because they don't know their worth, but because they can't see the full cost of a project until it's already over.

You quote based on gut. Then you finish the job and realize you didn't account for revision rounds, tool subscriptions, the Zoom calls that ate 4 hours, or the tax hit at the end of the year.

That's not a pricing problem. It's a visibility problem.

Before you send your next quote, run the numbers properly:

• What's the actual time cost (including admin, not just execution)?
• What are your tool and overhead costs per project?
• What rate do you need to hit your income goal after taxes?

Once you see the real cost of a project, your pricing shifts. Not because you got greedy — because you finally got honest.
