The client said "just a small tweak" — and then the project tripled in size.

No extra pay. No updated agreement. Just scope creep eating into your time while you smile and nod because nothing was ever written down.

That's not a client problem. That's a documentation problem.

A solid Scope of Work does three things:
1. Defines exactly what you're delivering (and what you're not)
2. Gives you something to point to when "small tweaks" appear
3. Sets the tone that you run a professional operation

Most freelancers skip it because it feels like extra admin work. But 30 minutes writing a clear SOW saves you hours of back-and-forth — and sometimes thousands of dollars in unpaid revisions.
