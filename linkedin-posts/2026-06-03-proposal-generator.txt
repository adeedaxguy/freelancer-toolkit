I lost a $8,000 project because my proposal looked like an afterthought.

The client went with someone else — not because they were cheaper, not because they were more experienced. They just "seemed more professional."

That stung. Because I knew I was the better fit.

Here's what I learned: clients make decisions emotionally first, then justify rationally. A polished proposal does that justification work for them before they even meet you.

What a strong proposal needs:
- A clear problem statement (show you actually listened)
- Defined deliverables with zero ambiguity
- Timeline, payment terms, and what you need from them

Most freelancers write proposals like emails. Clients who pay well expect something that looks like a business document.
