Upwork takes more than you think. Way more.

Most freelancers quote a rate, land the job, then do the mental math at invoice time — and wince.

Here's what actually happens:
- You quote $100/hr
- Upwork takes 10% (up to $500K in billings — but 20% below $500 with a client)
- You net $80–$90... before taxes

That gap quietly kills your effective hourly rate. A $75 project rate can feel like $60 by the time it hits your bank.

The fix isn't guessing. It's knowing your real take-home *before* you submit a proposal — so you price to win AND to profit.

I use the Upwork Fee Calculator at freeltools.com/tools/upwork-fee-calculator every time I'm scoping a new contract. Takes 10 seconds and saves the post-invoice regret.
