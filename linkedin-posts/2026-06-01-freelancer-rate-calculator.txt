Most freelancers undercharge — not because they lack confidence, but because they never actually did the math.

Here's what I mean:

You quote $75/hr thinking that's solid money. But after taxes, software subscriptions, unpaid admin time, sick days, and slow months — your real effective rate might be closer to $35/hr.

That's not a rate. That's a trap.

The calculation that actually matters:
→ What do you need to take home annually?
→ How many billable hours can you realistically work?
→ What's your overhead (tools, taxes, benefits)?
→ Add a buffer for the months clients disappear.

When you work backwards from those numbers, your "ambitious" rate suddenly becomes your minimum.

I ran mine through a freelance rate calculator and realized I had been leaving $20k+ on the table every year. Not because clients wouldn't pay — but because I never asked for it.
