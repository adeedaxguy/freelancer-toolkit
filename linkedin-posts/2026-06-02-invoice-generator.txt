I once lost a $4,200 client because my invoice looked like it was made in 2009.

Not kidding. They ghosted after I sent a PDF I'd cobbled together from a Word template. Later found out they went with someone else — "more professional."

That stung. But it taught me something: clients judge your business by every touchpoint. And the invoice is often the last impression you leave.

Here's what a good freelance invoice actually needs:
• Clear payment terms (Net 7, Net 15 — spell it out)
• Itemized line items, not just a lump sum
• Your business name/logo, not just your personal email
• A late fee clause — even if you never enforce it

Your invoice is a trust signal. Make it look like you run a real business — because you do.
