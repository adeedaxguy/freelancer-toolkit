## What causes rejections

Most rejected photos fail for practical reasons:

- The crop is too close or too far away.
- The background has shadows, texture, or objects.
- The face is tilted or not directly facing the camera.
- The image was compressed too aggressively.
- The printed photo was scaled by the printer instead of printed at actual size.

If your photo is being rejected by an online portal, start by regenerating it from the original image rather than editing the already-compressed file.

## Print and digital checks

Germany visa applicants may need a digital upload, printed photos, or both depending on the application path. These are different checks.

For digital upload:

- Confirm the accepted file type before uploading.
- Make sure the photo is not blurry after compression.
- Avoid screenshots of an already exported photo.
- Use the portal preview if one is available.

For printing:

- Print at actual size, not "fit to page."
- Use photo paper if the instructions require it.
- Do not crop the downloaded file again in the print dialog.
- Recheck the physical photo with a ruler if the print looks off.
