World Cup match times are easy to misread because the official listing, broadcaster listing, and fan's local time zone may all be different. A 3:00 PM kickoff in New Jersey is not a 3:00 PM kickoff for London, Dubai, Karachi, Delhi, Dhaka, or Sydney.

Use the [World Cup Match Time Converter](https://freeltools.com/tools/world-cup-match-time-converter) to convert kickoff times across countries and add the match to Google Calendar.

For official fixture confirmation, use [FIFA's match schedule](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/match-schedule). [AP's latest final coverage](https://apnews.com/article/argentina-messi-spain-yamal-world-cup-final-55077ce5c4728c4207a39cc4aa8a41a1) also places the 2026 final on Sunday, July 19 in East Rutherford, New Jersey.

## Quick answer

To convert a World Cup kickoff time:

1. Start with the official match date.
2. Note the source time zone, such as US Eastern.
3. Convert that time into UTC.
4. Add each destination country's UTC offset.
5. Save the result to your calendar.

The tool does that in one screen for the US, UK, Europe, UAE, Pakistan, India, Bangladesh, and Australia.

## Calendar workflow for watch parties

If you are planning a watch party, do this:

- Convert the match time first.
- Add the calendar event.
- Invite people using their own calendar addresses.
- Include the source fixture link in the description.
- Add a reminder 60 minutes before kickoff.
- Re-check the time if FIFA or the broadcaster updates the listing.

Do not rely on a screenshot from social media. It may show a different country, daylight-saving offset, or broadcaster schedule.

## Daylight saving caveat

The converter uses the listed offsets in the tool. For summer 2026, that includes common daylight-saving labels such as EDT, BST, and CEST.

That works for planning, but time-zone rules and fixture details can still create edge cases. For official plans, check the match listing and the calendar event before kickoff day.
