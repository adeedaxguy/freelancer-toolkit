## The Short Version

Use this formula for your baseline freelance hourly rate:

`baseline hourly rate = (target income + expenses + tax reserve + profit buffer) / annual billable hours`

Then use this formula for a fixed-price project:

`project price = estimated hours x baseline rate + direct costs + risk buffer + profit margin`

If you are selling through a marketplace, calculate net income after fees:

`net income = project price - platform fee - withdrawal fee - taxes - project expenses`

## Why the Employee Salary Shortcut Fails

If you want to earn $80,000 per year, you cannot simply divide $80,000 by 2,080 full-time work hours and charge $38 per hour. Freelancers do not get paid for every hour they work.

Unpaid work includes:

- Sales calls
- Proposal writing
- Follow-up emails
- Admin
- Accounting
- Portfolio updates
- Learning time
- Client onboarding
- Project management
- Downtime between clients

If you bill only 1,000 to 1,300 hours per year, the rate must be much higher than an employee hourly equivalent. That is why many freelancers need to charge **2-3x the employee hourly rate** to build a stable business.

## Add Platform Fees Before You Quote

A freelance services pricing calculator should include marketplace fees. Otherwise, the quote looks profitable until the platform takes its share.

Example: you want to keep $800 from a gig and you model the platform fee at 20%.

`required gross = target net / (1 - fee rate)`

`$800 / 0.80 = $1,000`

If you quote $800, you may keep about $640 before taxes. If you quote $1,000, you are closer to the intended $800 net before taxes.

## Pricing by Service Type

Different services need different pricing logic.

| Service Type | Best Pricing Model | Why |
|---|---|---|
| One-off design task | Fixed price | Clear deliverable and limited risk |
| SEO campaign | Monthly retainer | Ongoing work and reporting rhythm |
| Development sprint | Milestone pricing | Reduces risk across phases |
| Consulting call | Hourly or package | Time-bound value delivery |
| Rush project | Fixed price plus rush fee | Protects schedule and opportunity cost |

Hourly pricing is useful internally. Fixed pricing is often better externally. Retainers are best when the client needs recurring outcomes and you can define a monthly scope.

## Checklist Before Sending a Quote

- Did you include discovery time?
- Did you include project management time?
- Did you include revision rounds?
- Did you include a deadline or rush buffer?
- Did you include platform fees?
- Did you include payment processing fees?
- Did you reserve for taxes?
- Did you define what is not included?
- Did you add payment milestones?
- Did you create a scope of work?

If any answer is no, pause before sending the quote. Underpricing usually happens before the proposal, not after the client accepts.

## FAQ

### What is the best freelance pricing calculator download?

The best calculator is one that includes income goals, business expenses, tax reserve, billable hours, platform fees, project risk, and profit margin. A simple hourly-rate worksheet is useful, but it is incomplete without project and fee calculations.

### How much should I charge as a freelancer in 2026?

Start with your required annual revenue divided by realistic billable hours. Then adjust for niche, proof, demand, complexity, platform fees, and the value of the result. Many freelancers need to charge 2-3x an employee-equivalent hourly rate to stay profitable.

### Should I price hourly or fixed?

Use hourly pricing for open-ended support, consulting, or unclear scope. Use fixed pricing when deliverables, timeline, revisions, and acceptance criteria are clear. Use retainers for recurring outcomes.

### How do I include Fiverr or Upwork fees in my price?

Calculate the net amount you need first, then divide by one minus the fee rate. If you need $800 and expect a 20% fee, the gross price is $1,000. Always verify current platform fees inside your account.
