## How to get a cleaner PDF

The quality of the final PDF depends mostly on the images you start with. A good workflow:

1. Remove duplicates before uploading.
2. Crop out unnecessary borders or desk background.
3. Put the pages in the right order before export.
4. Review the PDF once on desktop or mobile.

If the images contain text, avoid over-compressing them first. Aggressive compression can make screenshots and scanned letters harder to read after they are placed inside a PDF.

## FAQ

### How do I combine multiple images into one PDF?

Use the [Image to PDF Converter](/tools/image-to-pdf-converter), upload the files in the right order, preview them, and export one PDF.

### Is an image-to-PDF converter better than a JPG-to-PDF converter?

It is better when your files are mixed. If every file is JPG, the [JPG to PDF Converter](/tools/jpg-to-pdf-converter) is fine. If the batch includes PNG or WebP files, the mixed-image converter is easier.

### Why is my PDF file so large?

Large phone photos and scans create large PDFs. Compress or resize the images before converting, especially when you are uploading the final PDF to a portal with a file-size limit.

### Will the PDF keep my page order?

Yes, if you arrange the images before export. Always open the downloaded PDF once to confirm the pages stayed in the correct sequence.
