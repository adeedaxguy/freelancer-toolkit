A $1,000 freelance project is not always worth $1,000. On a direct client project, you may keep the full gross amount before taxes and payment processing. On a marketplace, platform fees can reduce your take-home before the money reaches your account.

That is why a **freelancer fees calculator** is useful before you bid, accept a gig, or compare platforms.

Use the [Freelancer Fees Calculator](/tools/freelancer-commission-calculator) to compare platforms on your own project amount.

---

## Why gross revenue is misleading

Gross revenue is what the client pays. Net revenue is what you keep after platform deductions.

Example on a $1,000 order:

| Platform | Example fee | Estimated net |
|---|---:|---:|
| Direct client | 0% | $1,000 |
| Fiverr | 20% | $800 |
| Freelancer.com | 10% or $5 minimum | $900 |
| Upwork | 0-15% by contract | $850-$1,000 |

That gap changes your effective hourly rate. If a $1,000 project takes 10 hours, the gross rate is $100/hour. But after a 20% platform fee, the net rate is $80/hour before taxes.

---

## How to compare freelance platforms

Do not compare platforms only by percentage. Compare the full economics:

- Platform fee
- Proposal cost
- Win rate
- Average project size
- Client quality
- Time spent bidding
- Payment protection
- Whether the client can become repeat work

A platform with a higher fee can still be worth it if it brings higher-quality clients. A low-fee platform can still be poor if you spend too much time chasing underpriced work.

---

## How to price around platform fees

Start with the amount you need to keep, then gross it up.

Formula:

**Target net / (1 - fee rate) = quote amount**

If you need to keep $1,000:

- 5% fee: quote about $1,053
- 10% fee: quote about $1,111
- 15% fee: quote about $1,176
- 20% fee: quote $1,250

Use the platform fee calculator before sending the proposal so you do not discover the shortfall after the project is accepted.

## Direct clients still matter

Freelance platforms can be excellent acquisition channels, especially early on. But direct clients usually give you more control over pricing, communication, scope, and repeat work.

The best approach for many freelancers is blended:

- Use marketplaces for discovery and credibility
- Build repeat clients into retainers
- Ask happy clients for referrals
- Grow a direct pipeline over time
