## Decision table

| Input | Question to ask | Risk if skipped |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hours | How long will delivery take? | Base cost is unknown |
| Rate | What is your minimum healthy rate? | Quote may not support income target |
| Revisions | How much feedback time is included? | Scope creep starts early |
| Buffer | What could go wrong? | Fixed price absorbs surprises |

## Step-by-step workflow

1. Estimate the delivery hours honestly.
2. Use a rate that covers taxes and expenses.
3. Add planned revision time.
4. Add a 15 to 25 percent scope buffer for clear projects.
5. Send the quote with scope boundaries, not just a number.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Do not copy a competitor number without checking your own inputs.
- Do not treat a calculator result as final if the underlying assumption is wrong.
- Do not skip the follow-through: proposal, scope, audit fix, file check, or submission check.
- Do not optimize only for the cheapest or smallest number if quality, compliance, or margin matters.

## FAQ

### What is the simplest project price formula?

Estimated hours times rate, plus revision time, plus scope buffer.

### Should every project have a buffer?

Most fixed-price projects should. Reduce the buffer only when scope is very clear and risk is low.

### What tool should I open after the calculator?

Use the Proposal Generator or Scope of Work Generator to explain the price.
